Despite his departure from Shady Records, 50 Cent still snags an appearance on the Shady-heavy Southpaw soundtrack with the hard-hitting “Drama Never Ends.” This is his second solo song in 2015, following his album single, “Get Low.”

In the movie, 50 Cent plays the role of Jordan Mains, a boxing promoter who helps Jake Gyllenhaal’s role, the welterweight boxer Billy Hope. This somewhat parallels his real life persona as he founded the boxing promotion company SMS Promotions, though that filed for bankruptcy earlier in the year.

While the track itself isn’t thematically related to boxing, 50 Cent has been a major boxing fan since he was a kid. He started boxing when he was 11, and also participated in the Junior Olympics during the mid-80s.

When I wasn’t killing time in school, I was sparring in the gym or selling crack on the strip — I was competitive in the ring and hip-hop is competitive too … I think rappers condition themselves like boxers, so they all kind of feel like they’re the champ.
