Lil Wayne’s criticism of Bush’s oppression of the African-Americans of the U.S. during the Katrina tragedy. As well, that everyone must persevere through harsh times.

This song is often used as an example of what Wayne could be capable of if he tackled more difficult subject matter than money and girls more consistently.

As Drew Correa explained to XXL:

[T]hat is the oldest record on Tha Carter III. [Wayne] did that whole record in one take. He was smoking a blunt, and he’s like, “Yo just play it all the way through and I’m-a get all three verses in one shot.” He really felt attached to that record. He’s a huge Robin Thicke fan, and it was so deep to him, and he’s talking about Katrina. When we were on his tour bus going from venues he’d be in there jamming that record.
