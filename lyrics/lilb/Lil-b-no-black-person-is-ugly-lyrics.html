Lil B is best known for rapping about everything he can think of. With “No Black Person Is Ugly” he is providing us with his pure and ‘deep’ side.

Pitchfork was extremely positive of this side of Lil B.

No Black Person Is Ugly is one such moment—an empathic sentiment delivered loosely, a mood big and vague enough to live in. He opens with in a tightly packed flow that tricks you into expecting a linear, coherent performance. But you soon realize it’s Lil B straight through, a string of small, disconnected sentences tracing the contours of some big, connected thoughts.
