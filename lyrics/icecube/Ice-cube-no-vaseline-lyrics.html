“No Vaseline” is found on Ice Cube’s second album, Death Certificate. It was produced by Cube and Sir Jinx.

The song was a diss track aimed towards Cube’s former bandmates in N.W.A and their manager, Jerry Heller, in response to N.W.A.’s disses on their first two releases without him. The first minute of “No Vaseline” references N.W.A’s “Message to B.A.”, in which they call Ice Cube *“Benedict Arnold. Cube’s wordplay, airing of dirty laundry, and passionate anger made this one of the most celebrated battle rap songs ever.

Cube addresses Eazy-E and Heller with particularly harsh words, accusing them of unfairly exploiting the rest of the group. About a year after this song was released, Dre split from N.W.A. and Ruthless Records for many of the same reasons Cube bounced, co-founding Death Row Records with former bodyguard Marion “Suge” Knight and finding massive success with his multiplatinum solo album, The Chronic. Dre and his new protégé Snoop Doggy Dogg had plenty to say about Eazy and Heller on wax and in music videos.

“No Vaseline” was later included on the Death Row Greatest Hits compilation album, after Dre had split with Knight. It’s widely believed Knight included the track (and a related EP) as a dig at his ex-partner. The Death Row version omits the minute long intro and censors the word “Jew.”

On a related note, the UK release of Death Certificate omitted this song altogether, along with the 46-second long “Black Korea.”

The original sample is from “Dazz” (aka “Disco-Jazz”) by Brick.
